A gathering is any service or event your church holds.

That might be a Sunday morning service, a mid-week prayer night, a worship rehearsal, a baptism service, or a special event. If your church gathers for it, it can live in WorshipOS as a gathering.

## Creating a gathering

When you create a gathering, you give it a name, a date and time, and optionally a description. That is enough to get started.

From there, you build the **Worship Flow** — the plan for what will happen and who will be serving.

See [Worship Flow](/docs/worship-flow) for how flows work.

## The gathering lifecycle

Most gatherings move through a few natural stages:

1. **Planning** — the flow is being built, volunteers are being scheduled
2. **Confirmed** — the team is set, the plan is finalized
3. **Live** — the gathering is happening; adjustments may be made in real time
4. **Complete** — the gathering has ended; what actually happened is recorded alongside what was planned

The distinction between planned and actual matters. A song that got skipped, a prayer that ran long, a moment that wasn't on the plan — those are part of what the church experienced. WorshipOS preserves both the original plan and the reality of the day.

## Scheduling volunteers

Within a gathering, you assign people to the roles needed for the service.

Common roles include:

- Worship leader
- Vocals
- Guitar, keys, bass, drums
- Sound
- Slides / projection
- Video
- Welcome and ushers
- Kids ministry coverage

When you assign someone to a role, WorshipOS can check their availability and let you see how recently they have served. The goal is not to fill every slot automatically — it is to give leaders the information they need to make good decisions.

## Repeating gatherings

If your church holds services on a regular schedule, you can set up recurring gatherings rather than creating each one from scratch. The structure carries forward, and you adjust the specifics week by week.
