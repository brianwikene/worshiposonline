WorshipOS is a planning and coordination platform designed for churches.

The goal is to help leaders care for people, plan gatherings, and coordinate volunteers — without the overhead of tools built for corporate project management.

Most church software starts with logistics. WorshipOS starts with a different assumption: **people come first, and the system should support the humans doing the work**.

## How the system is organized

WorshipOS is built around three connected areas: **People**, **Gatherings**, and **Songs**. Everything else — groups, volunteers, check-in — extends from these foundations.

### People

People records represent the individuals in your church. Each record can include contact information, household relationships, volunteer roles, and notes about ministry involvement.

People connect to nearly everything else in the system. When you schedule a volunteer for a gathering, assign someone to a group, or check in a family on Sunday morning, you are working with People records.

See [People](/docs/people) for details.

### Gatherings

A gathering is any service or event your church holds — a Sunday morning, a mid-week prayer night, a rehearsal, a conference.

Each gathering has a **Worship Flow** where you plan the order of service, assign volunteers to roles, and track what actually happened versus what was planned.

See [Gatherings](/docs/gatherings) for details.

### Songs

The Songs library stores the music your church uses in worship. Songs include lyrics, chord charts, and arrangements, and can be inserted directly into a Worship Flow when planning a service.

See [Songs](/docs/songs) for details.

## Supporting tools

Beyond the three core areas, WorshipOS includes tools that help churches coordinate ministry:

- **Groups** — organize small groups, ministry teams, and volunteer teams within your congregation
- **Volunteers** — manage serving roles, track availability, and build schedules
- **Check-In** — track attendance at gatherings and follow up with care

These tools are designed to support the core work, not to become the center of it.

## What WorshipOS is not

WorshipOS is not a general-purpose church management system. It is not a financial platform, a giving tracker, or a communication broadcast tool.

It is built specifically for the people who plan and lead worship — and the teams who make it happen week after week.
