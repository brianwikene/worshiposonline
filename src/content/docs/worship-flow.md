The Worship Flow is the plan for a service — what happens, in what order, and who is responsible for each part.

Instead of coordinating across a shared document, a printed order of service, and a series of messages, the entire plan lives in one place. Everyone on the team sees the same thing.

## Building a flow

A Worship Flow is made up of **elements** — the individual moments that make up the service.

Common elements include:

- Welcome
- Song
- Scripture reading
- Announcements
- Offering
- Message
- Response time
- Prayer
- Closing

You can add elements in any order, reorder them by dragging, and customize the names to match your church's language. Not every church uses the same vocabulary for what happens on Sunday morning.

## Assigning people to elements

Each element can have one or more people assigned to it.

For a song, that might mean:

- Worship leader (lead)
- Vocals
- Guitar
- Keys
- Bass
- Drums
- Sound engineer
- Projection / slides

For a message, it might simply be the speaker's name.

Assignments appear on the shared flow, so every team member can see who is responsible for what without sending a separate communication.

## Songs in the flow

When you add a song element, you can link it directly to a song in your [Songs](/docs/songs) library. This connects the element to the song's lyrics, chord chart, and arrangement details — giving musicians easy access to what they need to prepare.

## Planned vs. actual

Services do not always go exactly as planned.

A prayer lasts longer than expected. A song gets skipped. A testimony emerges that was not on the schedule. A leader senses the need to change direction.

Those moments are not mistakes. They are part of what the church experienced together.

WorshipOS preserves both versions: what was planned before the service, and what actually happened. The original plan reflects careful preparation. The actual record reflects the reality of the day. Healthy teams learn from both.

## Sharing the flow

Once the flow is built and volunteers are assigned, your entire team can view it. There is no need to export a PDF or send a separate order of service email.

Everyone involved in the gathering — from the worship leader to the sound tech — can see the plan, their role, and any notes relevant to their part.
