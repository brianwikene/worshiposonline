Groups help churches organize the smaller communities that exist within the congregation.

A church is not a single undifferentiated crowd. It is made up of households, teams, ministries, and relationships — people connected to each other through shared purpose and shared life. Groups give those connections a place to exist in WorshipOS.

## What groups represent

Groups can represent nearly any organized subset of your church:

- **Small groups** — home groups, life groups, community groups
- **Ministry teams** — worship team, kids ministry, welcome team, AV crew
- **Leadership groups** — elders, deacons, staff, ministry leaders
- **Interest or affinity groups** — men's group, women's group, young adults

The structure is flexible. Groups are what you make them.

## Group membership

People can belong to multiple groups simultaneously. A person on the worship team might also be in a small group and on the welcome team.

Membership records can include a role within the group (leader, member, apprentice) to help distinguish levels of involvement without creating separate records.

## What groups are used for

Groups primarily help leaders:

- **See who belongs to what** — quickly understand how the congregation is organized
- **Track participation** — notice when group attendance patterns shift
- **Coordinate serving** — some groups exist specifically to manage a ministry team's scheduling

Groups connect to People records. When you view a person's profile, you can see all the groups they belong to alongside their serving history and household information.

## Groups and volunteers

For ministry teams that serve at gatherings — worship, AV, kids, welcome — the group can serve as the pool of available volunteers when scheduling a service.

Rather than searching all of People for someone with the right role, you can draw from the relevant group.

See [Volunteers](/docs/volunteers) for how scheduling and availability work.
