Most churches are using WorshipOS for real services within a few hours of signing up.

The key is to start small. You do not need to import your entire congregation before planning your first gathering. Begin with the people who will use it this week, then expand from there.

## Step 1 — Set up your church

When you first sign in, you will be asked to enter a few basics:

- Church name
- Subdomain (e.g., `grace.worshipos.com`)
- Timezone

Your timezone matters for scheduling. Make sure it reflects where your church is located.

## Step 2 — Invite your core team

Start with the 3–5 people most involved in planning gatherings:

- Worship leader
- Tech or AV lead
- Service coordinator or administrator
- Any ministry leaders who will be scheduling volunteers

You can add the rest of your team once the first gathering is planned. Most staff and volunteers do not need accounts — they receive scheduling requests and can respond without logging in.

## Step 3 — Add your first gathering

Create the next service on your calendar. It can be as simple as:

> Sunday Gathering — 10:00 AM

Once the gathering exists, your team can begin building the Worship Flow and assigning roles.

## Step 4 — Add a few songs

Add the songs you are planning to use in the next service. You do not need to build out your full library right away — start with what you need this week.

Songs can include lyrics, chord charts, and arrangement notes. Once they are in the library, they can be inserted directly into any Worship Flow.

## Step 5 — Build the Worship Flow

Add the elements that make up the service in order — songs, Scripture, announcements, message, prayer — and assign the person responsible for each part.

When the flow is complete, your whole team can see the same plan. No more coordinating over a shared document or emailing a PDF.

## Expanding over time

Once your team is comfortable with the core flow, you can add:

- Additional people and households
- Volunteer roles and availability
- Groups for small groups and ministry teams
- Check-In for Sunday attendance

There is no rush. The goal is for the system to become useful quickly, not comprehensive immediately.
